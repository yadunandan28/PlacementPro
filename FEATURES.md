# Modules / Features

PlacementPro helps students prepare for placements and lets staff guide and monitor them. Below is what the product offers, described by area.

---

## Account and access

- New users can sign up and sign in securely.
- Returning users stay signed in across visits until they sign out.
- Students choose a **cohort** (their batch or track) so their experience matches their group.
- **Students** use the learning and practice areas.
- **Staff** use dashboards to see students, progress, and interviews.
- **Admins** can add staff accounts so the team can manage the platform.

---

## Student dashboard

- A single home view shows how the student is doing overall.
- Scores are shown by area: problem solving (coding), operating systems, databases, and networking.
- Progress includes how many coding problems they have cleared, how many learning modules they have finished, and how mock interviews have gone when available.
- Simple charts make it easy to see strengths and weaker areas at a glance.

---

## Coding practice

- Students work through a library of programming questions.
- They can browse and open questions that fit what they want to practice.
- They write and run their solution in the app and submit it for checking.
- The system records their attempts and whether they solved each problem, so progress shows up on the dashboard.

---

## Multiple-choice (MCQ) practice

- Students can practice topic questions in a quiz style.
- They can focus by subject and difficulty so practice matches their goals.
- Each attempt is tracked so effort and improvement feed into their overall picture.

---

## Learning modules

- Each cohort has a path of **modules**: ordered steps with short descriptions and optional learning resources.
- Each module ends with a quiz. Students must reach a minimum score to **pass** the module.
- Passing unlocks completion for that module and updates their progress summary.
- Staff define what each module contains and how high the bar is to pass.

---

## Subject assessments

- Formal assessments cover core subjects: operating systems, database management, and computer networks.
- Students answer a fixed set of questions for that subject and submit in one go.
- They receive a score and feedback-style messaging based on how well they did.
- Their **best** score in each subject is kept so they can see improvement over time and staff can see readiness.

---

## Profile and resume

- Students can view and update their profile information.
- They can upload a **resume** so it is stored with their account for placement-related use.

---

## Job description assistant (chat)

- Students upload a **job description** (as a document the app accepts).
- The assistant reads it and suggests **focus areas**: what to emphasize and what to strengthen for that role.
- Students can chat naturally—study tips, interview prep, skill gaps—and answers stay relevant to that job description.
- They can clear the session and start fresh with a different role or company.

---

## Mock interviews

- **Staff** schedule mock interviews and assign them to students.
- Students see upcoming and past interviews in their own list.
- During an interview, they answer **spoken** questions (their answers are captured through the app) and **coding** questions when those appear.
- They can run and submit code as part of the interview flow.
- When finished, they get a **score** or result summary for that session.
- Students may **upload a recording** of the session so staff can review how they performed.
- **Staff** can see results and recordings to give feedback and track readiness.

---

## Staff dashboard and reporting

- Staff see an overview of activity and performance across the platform or by cohort.
- They can browse and search the student list and open a **detail view** for one student: scores by area, attempts, module progress, and interview-related outcomes where available.
- Charts support quick comparisons and spotting cohorts or individuals who need support.
- Staff can **export** a report for offline review or sharing with the team.

---

## Content and structure (staff)

- Staff maintain the **question bank**: both programming and multiple-choice items, including updates and retiring old questions.
- Staff define **cohorts** and the **modules** inside each cohort—order, descriptions, resources, and pass rules for module quizzes.
- This keeps the learning path aligned with what the placement cell wants students to cover.
